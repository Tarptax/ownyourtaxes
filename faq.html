Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common tax questions for small business owners, contractors, musicians, online sellers, and gig workers.

General Tax Questions

Why is tax education important?

Tax education helps individuals and business owners make informed financial decisions throughout the year instead of only thinking about taxes at filing time. A basic understanding of tax rules can help you stay organized, plan ahead, avoid common mistakes, and better understand how financial decisions may affect your tax situation.

What records should I keep for tax purposes?

Good recordkeeping is the foundation of accurate tax reporting. Taxpayers should generally keep records of income, expenses, receipts, invoices, bank statements, mileage logs, and any tax forms received throughout the year. Organized records can make tax preparation easier and provide documentation if questions arise later.

How long should I keep tax records?

Many taxpayers keep important tax records for several years after filing a return. The appropriate retention period can vary based on individual circumstances and the types of records involved. When in doubt, maintaining organized records for an extended period can provide peace of mind and help support information reported on past tax returns.

Small Business Owners

What expenses can a small business deduct?

Many small businesses incur expenses that are ordinary and necessary for operating the business. Common examples may include office supplies, software subscriptions, advertising, insurance, professional services, equipment, and business-related travel. Maintaining accurate records throughout the year can help business owners better understand their expenses and stay organized.

Do I need a separate bank account for my business?

While requirements can vary, keeping business and personal finances separate is often a good practice. A dedicated business bank account can simplify bookkeeping, make it easier to track income and expenses, and help reduce confusion when preparing financial records and tax information.

What tax records should a business owner track?

Business owners should maintain organized records of income, expenses, receipts, invoices, bank statements, mileage logs, payroll records, and other documents related to business activity. Consistent recordkeeping not only supports tax reporting but can also provide valuable insight into the financial health of the business.

Independent Contractors

Do contractors pay taxes differently than employees?

Independent contractors are typically responsible for tracking their own income, maintaining business records, and handling their tax obligations throughout the year. Unlike employees, taxes are generally not withheld from contractor payments, making recordkeeping and financial planning especially important.

Can contractors deduct mileage?

Vehicle expenses related to business activities may qualify for tax deductions when properly documented. Many contractors maintain detailed mileage logs to track travel associated with client meetings, job sites, and other business purposes. Good documentation is an important part of maintaining accurate records.

Why is expense tracking important?

Expense tracking helps contractors understand profitability, monitor cash flow, and maintain accurate financial records throughout the year. Consistent recordkeeping can also simplify tax preparation and make it easier to identify legitimate business expenses.

Gig Workers

Do gig workers need to report income?

Income earned from gig work is generally considered taxable income and should be tracked throughout the year. Keeping accurate records of income, expenses, and mileage can make tax filing easier and help you stay organized. Many gig workers benefit from maintaining records throughout the year rather than trying to reconstruct them during tax season.

What expenses might gig workers track?

Depending on the type of work performed, gig workers often track mileage, cell phone usage, supplies, equipment, platform fees, and other business-related expenses. Maintaining organized records throughout the year can make it easier to understand expenses, monitor profitability, and prepare accurate tax information when needed.

Why is recordkeeping important for gig workers?

Consistent recordkeeping helps gig workers stay organized and avoid scrambling for information during tax season. Keeping records throughout the year can make it easier to track income, document expenses, monitor business performance, and maintain accurate financial information.

Musicians & Creative Professionals

Should musicians keep records of equipment purchases?

Maintaining records for instruments, equipment, software, recording tools, and other business purchases can be an important part of staying organized for tax purposes. Keeping receipts and documentation throughout the year can help musicians maintain accurate financial records and better understand their business expenses.

Can musicians track travel and performance expenses?

Many musicians and creative professionals maintain records of travel, lodging, equipment transportation, rehearsal expenses, and other costs connected to their professional activities. Organized documentation can make it easier to track business-related spending and prepare financial information throughout the year.

Why is bookkeeping important for musicians?

Musicians often earn income from multiple sources, including performances, teaching, streaming, royalties, merchandise sales, and freelance work. Consistent bookkeeping can help track income, monitor expenses, stay organized, and provide a clearer picture of overall financial performance.

Online Sellers

Should online sellers track inventory purchases?

Maintaining records of inventory purchases, shipping costs, marketplace fees, and other business expenses can help online sellers stay organized throughout the year. Accurate records provide valuable insight into business operations and can make financial reporting much easier.

What financial records should online sellers keep?

Online sellers often maintain records of sales reports, inventory purchases, marketplace fees, shipping expenses, advertising costs, bank statements, and other business transactions. Organized records can help sellers understand profitability, monitor business performance, and prepare accurate financial information.

Why is bookkeeping important for ecommerce businesses?

Good bookkeeping helps ecommerce business owners understand revenue, control expenses, monitor cash flow, and measure profitability. Consistent recordkeeping can also make it easier to identify trends, evaluate business decisions, and maintain organized financial records throughout the year.

Why should online sellers review their records regularly?

Regularly reviewing financial records can help online sellers identify trends, monitor inventory-related costs, track fees charged by selling platforms, and make more informed business decisions throughout the year.
