What Records to Keep & How Long

One of the most common questions business owners, contractors, freelancers, and self-employed individuals ask is how long they should keep financial and tax records.

Maintaining organized records can make tax season easier, support financial management, and help ensure important documents are available when needed. Creating a simple record retention system can save significant time and frustration in the future.

Why Record Retention Matters

Keeping important records can help you:

  • Prepare tax returns more efficiently.
  • Verify financial transactions.
  • Support business recordkeeping.
  • Review business performance.
  • Locate important documents when needed.

A well-organized record retention system often makes financial management much easier throughout the year.

Income Records

Examples include:

  • Forms W-2
  • Forms 1099
  • Invoices
  • Sales reports
  • Payment records
  • Deposit records

These documents help support income reporting and can be useful for future reference.

Expense Records

Examples include:

  • Receipts
  • Invoices
  • Business purchase records
  • Advertising expenses
  • Software subscriptions
  • Professional service expenses

Maintaining organized expense documentation can make financial reviews and tax preparation easier.

Bank & Credit Card Records

Consider retaining:

  • Bank statements
  • Credit card statements
  • Deposit records
  • Electronic payment confirmations

These records can help verify transactions and support financial organization.

Mileage & Vehicle Records

Vehicle records may include:

  • Mileage logs
  • Vehicle maintenance records
  • Fuel receipts
  • Insurance documentation
  • Repair records

Organized vehicle records are often easier to maintain throughout the year than trying to reconstruct them later.

Tax-Related Records

Examples include:

  • Tax returns
  • Estimated tax payment records
  • IRS correspondence
  • State tax notices
  • Supporting documentation

Many taxpayers find it helpful to maintain a dedicated folder for tax documents by year.

Business Records

Important business records may include:

  • Business licenses
  • Insurance policies
  • Business registrations
  • Contracts and agreements
  • Ownership documents

Some business records may need to be maintained long after the related tax year has ended.

Digital vs. Paper Records

Many businesses now maintain records electronically. Digital storage can help reduce clutter and make documents easier to locate.

Whether you choose paper records, digital records, or a combination of both, consistency is often more important than the specific format used.

Create a Record Retention System

Consider organizing records by:

  • Year
  • Income records
  • Expense records
  • Bank statements
  • Tax documents
  • Business records

A simple filing system can make retrieving information much easier when needed.

Common Recordkeeping Mistakes

Throwing Records Away Too Soon

Many people discard documents without considering whether future access may be necessary.

Keeping Everything Without Organization

Saving documents is helpful, but documents should also be organized in a way that allows them to be located efficiently.

Ignoring Digital Backups

Maintaining backup copies of important records can help prevent information loss.

Waiting Until Tax Season

Recordkeeping is usually easier when performed throughout the year rather than at the last minute.

Best Practice

When in doubt, maintain important financial and tax records in an organized and secure location. A simple filing system combined with regular backups can help protect valuable information and simplify future record retrieval.

The Bottom Line

Good record retention is an important part of financial organization. By maintaining organized records for income, expenses, taxes, banking, and business activities, you can make tax season easier and improve your overall financial management.

The best recordkeeping system is the one that helps you locate important information quickly, accurately, and consistently.
