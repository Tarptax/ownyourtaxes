Tax Season Preparation Checklist

A little preparation before tax season can save significant time, reduce stress, and make the filing process much smoother. Use this checklist to help ensure that important records and information are organized before filing deadlines arrive.

Whether you are a small business owner, independent contractor, freelancer, gig worker, or online seller, consistent preparation can help you approach tax season with greater confidence.

Income Records

  • □ Gather all income records.
  • □ Review Forms W-2, 1099, and other income documents.
  • □ Verify income records against personal records.
  • □ Check for missing income documents.
  • □ Organize income records by source.

Business Expense Records

  • □ Organize expense receipts.
  • □ Review categorized business expenses.
  • □ Save invoices and supporting documentation.
  • □ Verify expense totals.
  • □ Identify any missing records.

Bank & Financial Records

  • □ Gather business bank statements.
  • □ Gather business credit card statements.
  • □ Reconcile account balances.
  • □ Review unusual transactions.
  • □ Confirm records are complete.

Mileage & Vehicle Records

  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Verify business travel records.
  • □ Organize vehicle expense documentation.
  • □ Review mileage totals for accuracy.

Estimated Tax Payments

  • □ Gather records of estimated tax payments.
  • □ Confirm payment dates.
  • □ Verify payment amounts.
  • □ Organize payment confirmations.

Tax Documents

  • □ Locate prior-year tax returns.
  • □ Save current-year tax forms.
  • □ Organize IRS correspondence.
  • □ Review any IRS notices received.

Digital Organization

  • □ Create a folder for the current tax year.
  • □ Back up important records.
  • □ Organize digital receipts and invoices.
  • □ Store copies of tax-related documents.

Before Filing

  • □ Review income records for completeness.
  • □ Review expense records for accuracy.
  • □ Verify mileage records.
  • □ Confirm estimated tax payment records.
  • □ Identify missing information.
  • □ Organize records in a single location.

After Filing

  • □ Save a copy of the filed return.
  • □ Save supporting documentation.
  • □ Store records securely.
  • □ Review opportunities to improve recordkeeping next year.

Quick Tax Season Readiness Check

  • □ Income records organized
  • □ Expense records organized
  • □ Mileage records complete
  • □ Bank statements reviewed
  • □ Estimated tax payments verified
  • □ Tax forms collected
  • □ Prior-year return available
  • □ Documents backed up

Tip: Tax season is often easier when preparation occurs throughout the year. Small organizational habits can dramatically reduce last-minute stress and make recordkeeping more manageable.
