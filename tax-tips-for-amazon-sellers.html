Tax Tips for Amazon Sellers

Selling on Amazon can create exciting opportunities for entrepreneurs, whether they are selling a handful of products each month or operating a growing online business. As sales increase, so do the recordkeeping and organizational responsibilities that come with running an ecommerce business.

Understanding basic tax and recordkeeping concepts can help Amazon sellers stay organized, monitor business performance, and prepare for tax season more effectively.

Treat Your Amazon Business Like a Business

Many sellers begin by listing a few products and gradually grow into a larger operation. Regardless of size, maintaining business records from the beginning can make managing growth much easier.

Strong business habits often include:

  • Income tracking
  • Expense organization
  • Inventory management
  • Monthly financial reviews
  • Recordkeeping systems

Track All Business Income

Amazon sellers should maintain organized records of income throughout the year.

Income records may include:

  • Amazon marketplace sales
  • Direct customer sales
  • Website sales
  • Other ecommerce platform sales

Maintaining your own records can help you better understand business performance and verify information as needed.

Monitor Amazon Fees

One area many new sellers underestimate is the impact of marketplace fees.

Amazon-related costs may include:

  • Referral fees
  • Fulfillment fees
  • Storage fees
  • Subscription fees
  • Advertising expenses

Reviewing account statements regularly can help sellers understand how business costs affect overall profitability.

Maintain Inventory Records

Inventory management is one of the most important organizational responsibilities for many Amazon sellers.

Consider maintaining records for:

  • Inventory purchases
  • Product quantities
  • Inventory received
  • Inventory sold
  • Inventory storage activity

Organized inventory records can provide valuable insight into the business and support better planning decisions.

Save Receipts and Supporting Documentation

Many Amazon businesses generate a large number of transactions throughout the year.

Examples of documents worth organizing include:

  • Inventory purchase receipts
  • Shipping records
  • Packaging supply receipts
  • Advertising expenses
  • Software subscription records
  • Equipment purchases

Digital storage systems can help make these records easier to locate later.

Separate Business and Personal Finances

Maintaining separate accounts for business activity can simplify recordkeeping considerably.

Dedicated business accounts can help sellers:

  • Track income more accurately.
  • Monitor expenses.
  • Review business performance.
  • Prepare records for tax season.

Mixing personal and business transactions often creates unnecessary complexity.

Understand Form 1099-K

Many Amazon sellers become familiar with Form 1099-K as their business grows.

Maintaining organized sales records and retaining copies of marketplace reports can help sellers compare information reported on tax forms to their own records.

Good recordkeeping remains important regardless of which forms are received.

Review Your Business Monthly

A monthly review can help Amazon sellers stay organized and identify issues before they become larger problems.

  • □ Review sales activity.
  • □ Review inventory levels.
  • □ Organize receipts.
  • □ Review advertising costs.
  • □ Categorize expenses.
  • □ Reconcile business accounts.
  • □ Back up important records.

Common Amazon Seller Mistakes

Ignoring Inventory Records

Inventory often represents a significant portion of an Amazon business. Poor inventory tracking can create organizational challenges.

Not Reviewing Amazon Fees

Marketplace fees can have a meaningful impact on profitability and should be reviewed regularly.

Mixing Business and Personal Finances

Combining transactions can make recordkeeping and financial reviews more difficult.

Waiting Until Tax Season

Trying to organize a year's worth of ecommerce activity at once can be overwhelming and time-consuming.

Amazon Seller Checklist

  • □ Track all sales activity.
  • □ Review marketplace fees.
  • □ Maintain inventory records.
  • □ Save receipts and documentation.
  • □ Separate business finances.
  • □ Review business performance monthly.
  • □ Back up important records.
  • □ Monitor tax planning needs.

The Bottom Line

Successful Amazon sellers often invest as much effort into organization and recordkeeping as they do into generating sales. Tracking income, monitoring inventory, reviewing fees, and maintaining accurate records can help create a stronger and more sustainable business.

The goal is not simply selling products—it is building a business that is organized, informed, and prepared for long-term success.
