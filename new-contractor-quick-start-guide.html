Starting work as an independent contractor can be exciting, but it also comes with new financial and recordkeeping responsibilities. Developing good habits early can make managing income, expenses, and taxes much easier in the future.

Use this guide as a starting point for building an organized foundation as a new contractor.

Step 1: Separate Business and Personal Finances

  • □ Open a dedicated business bank account.
  • □ Deposit contractor income into one account.
  • □ Avoid mixing business and personal purchases.
  • □ Keep business records separate from personal records.

Separating finances makes recordkeeping easier and provides a clearer picture of your business activity.

Step 2: Track Your Income

  • □ Record all payments received.
  • □ Save invoices and payment confirmations.
  • □ Compare deposits to your records regularly.
  • □ Maintain organized income documentation.

Do not rely solely on year-end tax forms to track your income.

Step 3: Create an Expense Tracking System

  • □ Save business receipts.
  • □ Create expense categories.
  • □ Track expenses consistently.
  • □ Review expenses each month.

Consistent tracking is often much easier than trying to reconstruct expenses later.

Step 4: Track Mileage

  • □ Create a mileage log.
  • □ Record business trips regularly.
  • □ Document the purpose of each trip.
  • □ Save vehicle-related records.

Developing a mileage tracking habit early can save significant time later.

Step 5: Learn the Basics of Estimated Taxes

  • □ Understand that taxes may not be withheld from payments.
  • □ Learn about estimated tax obligations.
  • □ Set aside money for future tax responsibilities.
  • □ Monitor tax deadlines throughout the year.

Step 6: Build a Recordkeeping System

  • □ Create folders for income records.
  • □ Create folders for expenses.
  • □ Save digital copies of important documents.
  • □ Back up records regularly.

Good recordkeeping habits can make tax season much less stressful.

Step 7: Establish a Monthly Routine

  • □ Review income.
  • □ Review expenses.
  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Organize receipts.
  • □ Reconcile bank accounts.
  • □ Review tax planning needs.

Small monthly reviews often prevent large organizational challenges later.

First-Year Success Checklist

  • □ Business and personal finances separated.
  • □ Income tracking system established.
  • □ Expense tracking system established.
  • □ Mileage logs maintained.
  • □ Tax planning process created.
  • □ Recordkeeping routine developed.
  • □ Important documents backed up.

The Bottom Line

Successful contractors often develop strong financial habits from the beginning. Tracking income, organizing expenses, maintaining records, and staying aware of tax responsibilities can make running your business much more manageable.

The goal is not perfection—it's creating simple systems that help you stay organized, informed, and prepared throughout the year.
