Travel Expenses For Performers

Many performers spend a significant amount of time traveling for their work. Musicians, actors, comedians, speakers, dancers, and other entertainers may travel to venues, rehearsals, auditions, recording sessions, conferences, and special events throughout the year.

Because travel often becomes a regular part of performance-related activities, maintaining organized travel records can be an important part of financial management and recordkeeping.

Why Travel Recordkeeping Matters

Travel activity can generate a large number of records over the course of a year.

Maintaining organized records can help performers:

  • Monitor business activity.
  • Track travel-related costs.
  • Stay organized throughout the year.
  • Review business profitability.
  • Prepare financial records more efficiently.

Common Types of Performance Travel

Travel needs vary depending on the profession and type of work being performed.

Examples may include:

  • Live performances
  • Tours
  • Rehearsals
  • Recording sessions
  • Auditions
  • Industry conferences
  • Workshops and training events
  • Client meetings

Maintain a Travel Log

One of the best organizational habits performers can develop is maintaining a travel log throughout the year.

Information commonly tracked may include:

  • Date of travel
  • Destination
  • Purpose of the trip
  • Associated event or performance
  • Supporting documentation

Maintaining records at the time of travel is often easier than attempting to reconstruct information later.

Save Transportation Records

Performers may use a variety of transportation methods throughout the year.

Consider organizing records related to:

  • Airfare
  • Train travel
  • Bus transportation
  • Rideshare services
  • Taxis
  • Rental vehicles
  • Parking fees
  • Tolls

Keeping transportation records together can make future reviews much easier.

Track Mileage

Many performers travel locally for rehearsals, performances, lessons, networking events, and business meetings.

When driving for business-related activities, maintaining a mileage log can be an important recordkeeping habit.

Mileage logs may include:

  • Date of travel
  • Business purpose
  • Starting mileage
  • Ending mileage
  • Total business miles

Organize Lodging Records

Travel often includes overnight stays for performances and events.

Maintaining organized lodging documentation can help create a complete travel record.

Examples may include:

  • Hotel confirmations
  • Receipts
  • Reservation records
  • Event schedules

Save Event Documentation

Travel records are often stronger when supported by documents related to the event itself.

Examples include:

  • Performance agreements
  • Contracts
  • Event schedules
  • Invoices
  • Performance confirmations

Keeping travel and event records together can improve overall organization.

Create a Digital Travel Folder

Many performers find it helpful to create digital folders for each trip or event.

A typical folder may contain:

  • Travel confirmations
  • Receipts
  • Contracts
  • Schedules
  • Payment records
  • Event information

Digital organization can significantly reduce the time required to locate important documents.

Review Travel Activity Monthly

Travel records can accumulate quickly.

A monthly review can help performers:

  • □ Organize receipts.
  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Review upcoming travel.
  • □ Save event documentation.
  • □ Back up important records.
  • □ Verify travel information is complete.

Common Travel Recordkeeping Mistakes

Waiting Until Tax Season

Travel details can be difficult to remember months after an event has occurred.

Losing Receipts

Travel often generates numerous documents that can quickly become disorganized without a system.

Ignoring Small Expenses

Parking fees, tolls, and other smaller costs can easily be forgotten if they are not recorded promptly.

Failing to Document Trip Purpose

Recording why travel occurred can help create a more complete record.

Performer Travel Checklist

  • □ Maintain a travel log.
  • □ Save transportation records.
  • □ Track mileage.
  • □ Organize lodging documentation.
  • □ Save performance agreements.
  • □ Store travel records digitally.
  • □ Review travel activity monthly.
  • □ Back up important documents.

The Bottom Line

Travel is often an essential part of a performer's career. Developing a system for tracking trips, organizing documents, and maintaining complete records can improve financial organization and make year-end reviews much easier.

The best approach is to create consistent habits throughout the year so that important information is available whenever it is needed.
