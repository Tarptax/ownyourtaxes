Business Recordkeeping Checklist

Use this checklist throughout the year to help keep your records organized and make tax season less stressful.

Income Records

  • □ Track all business income received.
  • □ Save invoices and customer payment records.
  • □ Reconcile income with bank deposits.
  • □ Retain Forms 1099 and other income documents.
  • □ Maintain records for all payment platforms used.

Expense Records

  • □ Save receipts for business purchases.
  • □ Organize expenses by category.
  • □ Retain invoices and bills.
  • □ Keep records of software subscriptions.
  • □ Track advertising and marketing expenses.
  • □ Review expenses monthly.

Banking & Financial Records

  • □ Maintain separate business and personal accounts.
  • □ Save monthly bank statements.
  • □ Save business credit card statements.
  • □ Review transactions for accuracy.
  • □ Reconcile accounts regularly.

Vehicle & Mileage Records

  • □ Record business mileage consistently.
  • □ Document the purpose of business trips.
  • □ Retain vehicle-related receipts.
  • □ Update mileage logs regularly.

Tax Planning

  • □ Set aside money for tax obligations.
  • □ Track estimated tax payments.
  • □ Maintain copies of filed tax returns.
  • □ Save IRS correspondence and notices.
  • □ Review tax records before year-end.

Digital Organization

  • □ Create folders for each tax year.
  • □ Store digital copies of important documents.
  • □ Back up financial records regularly.
  • □ Organize receipts and statements by month.
  • □ Secure sensitive financial information.

Monthly Review Checklist

  • □ Review income records.
  • □ Review expense records.
  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Verify bank transactions.
  • □ File receipts and documents.
  • □ Review upcoming tax deadlines.

Year-End Review Checklist

  • □ Verify income records are complete.
  • □ Organize all expense documentation.
  • □ Confirm estimated tax payment records.
  • □ Review bank and credit card statements.
  • □ Ensure mileage logs are complete.
  • □ Prepare records for tax season.

Tip: The best recordkeeping system is the one you actually use consistently. Small weekly efforts can prevent major headaches during tax season.
