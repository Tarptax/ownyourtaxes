Mileage Tracking for Gig Workers

For many gig workers, mileage is one of the most important aspects of business recordkeeping. Whether you drive for Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart, Spark, Roadie, Shipt, or multiple platforms, maintaining accurate mileage records can help you better understand your business activity and stay organized throughout the year.

Because gig work often involves significant driving, building a consistent mileage-tracking routine can become one of the most valuable recordkeeping habits you develop.

Why Mileage Tracking Matters

Many gig workers spend a considerable amount of time on the road while earning income.

Accurate mileage records can help:

  • Track business activity.
  • Maintain organized records.
  • Monitor vehicle usage.
  • Support financial reviews.
  • Prepare for tax season.

The best mileage records are typically created as trips occur rather than months later.

Who Should Track Mileage?

Mileage tracking can be beneficial for a variety of gig workers.

Examples may include:

  • Rideshare drivers
  • Food delivery drivers
  • Grocery delivery shoppers
  • Package delivery drivers
  • Independent couriers
  • Multi-app gig workers

Anyone who regularly drives as part of their business activity should consider maintaining organized mileage records.

What Should a Mileage Log Include?

A mileage log should be simple, consistent, and easy to maintain.

Common information may include:

  • Date of travel
  • Business purpose
  • Starting odometer reading
  • Ending odometer reading
  • Total miles driven

Keeping records current is generally easier than trying to reconstruct information later.

Track Mileage Consistently

One of the most common mistakes gig workers make is waiting until the end of the month—or even tax season—to estimate mileage.

Because gig work can involve frequent driving, small gaps in recordkeeping can quickly become difficult to recreate accurately.

Developing a daily tracking habit can help avoid these problems.

Multi-App Drivers Need Strong Records

Many gig workers operate on multiple platforms during the same day.

Examples may include:

  • Uber and Uber Eats
  • DoorDash and Instacart
  • Spark and Roadie
  • Multiple delivery platforms

Maintaining a single mileage system can help keep records organized across all business activities.

Save Supporting Vehicle Records

Mileage logs are often just one part of a broader vehicle recordkeeping system.

Consider organizing:

  • Fuel receipts
  • Maintenance records
  • Repair invoices
  • Insurance documents
  • Vehicle registration records

Keeping related records together can make future reviews easier.

Choose a Tracking Method

Gig workers use a variety of tracking methods.

Examples include:

  • Paper mileage logs
  • Spreadsheets
  • Digital tracking apps
  • Vehicle recordkeeping systems

The most effective system is usually the one you can maintain consistently.

Review Mileage Monthly

Regular reviews can help ensure records remain complete and organized.

  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Review business trips.
  • □ Organize vehicle records.
  • □ Save fuel receipts.
  • □ Review maintenance expenses.
  • □ Back up digital records.

Monthly reviews are often easier than year-end catch-up projects.

Common Mileage Tracking Mistakes

Waiting Until Tax Season

Trying to estimate months of driving activity can lead to inaccurate or incomplete records.

Relying on Memory

Business trips are easier to document at the time they occur than long after the fact.

Ignoring Small Trips

Short business trips can add up significantly over the course of a year.

Not Saving Supporting Records

Vehicle-related documents can help strengthen an overall recordkeeping system.

Mileage Tracking Checklist for Gig Workers

  • □ Track trips regularly.
  • □ Record dates and destinations.
  • □ Record beginning and ending mileage.
  • □ Maintain a consistent tracking method.
  • □ Save vehicle-related documents.
  • □ Review mileage monthly.
  • □ Organize vehicle records.
  • □ Back up important information.

The Bottom Line

Mileage tracking is one of the most important recordkeeping responsibilities for many gig workers. A consistent system can help improve organization, support financial reviews, and make tax season significantly easier.

The goal is not to create a complicated process. The goal is to develop a simple habit that keeps your records accurate, current, and easy to maintain throughout the year.
