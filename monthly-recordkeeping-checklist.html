Monthly Recordkeeping Checklist

Consistent recordkeeping is often easier than trying to organize an entire year's worth of financial activity during tax season. Use this monthly checklist to stay organized and keep your financial records up to date throughout the year.

A few minutes each month can help reduce stress, improve accuracy, and make tax preparation much easier.

Income Review

  • □ Record all income received during the month.
  • □ Verify deposits against your records.
  • □ Save invoices and payment confirmations.
  • □ Review any outstanding invoices.
  • □ Organize income records by source.

Expense Review

  • □ Record business expenses.
  • □ Save receipts and supporting documentation.
  • □ Categorize expenses consistently.
  • □ Review recurring subscriptions and fees.
  • □ Identify any uncategorized transactions.

Bank Account Review

  • □ Review business bank statements.
  • □ Verify deposits and withdrawals.
  • □ Compare records to bank activity.
  • □ Investigate unfamiliar transactions.
  • □ Reconcile account balances.

Credit Card Review

  • □ Review business credit card activity.
  • □ Save statements.
  • □ Categorize purchases.
  • □ Verify business-related transactions.
  • □ File supporting receipts.

Mileage & Vehicle Records

  • □ Update mileage logs.
  • □ Verify trip purposes are documented.
  • □ Save vehicle-related receipts.
  • □ Review mileage records for completeness.

Document Organization

  • □ File receipts and invoices.
  • □ Organize digital records.
  • □ Save email receipts.
  • □ Store documents in the appropriate folders.
  • □ Back up important records.

Tax Planning Review

  • □ Review year-to-date income.
  • □ Review year-to-date expenses.
  • □ Monitor estimated tax planning.
  • □ Check for upcoming deadlines.
  • □ Identify any missing documentation.

Business Health Check

  • □ Review monthly profitability.
  • □ Review major expenses.
  • □ Compare income trends.
  • □ Monitor cash flow.
  • □ Note any financial concerns that need attention.

End-of-Month Quick Review

  • □ Income recorded
  • □ Expenses categorized
  • □ Receipts saved
  • □ Bank accounts reviewed
  • □ Mileage updated
  • □ Documents organized
  • □ Records backed up
  • □ Tax planning reviewed

Tip: The goal is consistency, not perfection. Spending 15–30 minutes each month reviewing your records can prevent major organizational challenges during tax season.
